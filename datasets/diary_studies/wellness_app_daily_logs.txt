WELLNESS APP DIARY STUDY - DAILY LOGS
Study: Wellness App User Experience Over Time
Method: Diary study with daily prompts
Duration: 14 days
Date Range: March 2024

================================================================================
PARTICIPANT 5001 - Age: 29, Role: Marketing Manager
================================================================================

Day 1 - March 1, 2024
Prompt: "How did you use the wellness app today? What worked well? What didn't?"

"I downloaded the app this morning and set up my profile. The onboarding was pretty smooth - it asked me about my goals, my current activity level, stuff like that. I set a goal to walk 10,000 steps a day and meditate for 10 minutes. 

I went for a walk during lunch and the app tracked my steps automatically, which was cool. But I forgot to log my meditation, and when I tried to add it later, I couldn't figure out how. The interface is a bit confusing - there are a lot of buttons and I'm not sure what they all do.

Overall, first day was okay. I'm optimistic, but I need to figure out how to use all the features."

Day 2 - March 2, 2024
Prompt: "Did you remember to use the app? What motivated or prevented you?"

"I almost forgot to use it today. I was busy at work and didn't check the app until evening. I did hit my step goal though - walked to a coffee shop instead of driving. The app sent me a notification congratulating me, which was nice. Felt good to get that positive reinforcement.

I still haven't figured out how to log meditation. I tried meditating this morning but forgot to start a session in the app. By the time I remembered, I was already done. Maybe I need to set a reminder or something."

Day 3 - March 3, 2024
Prompt: "What's your biggest frustration with the app so far?"

"My biggest frustration is that I keep forgetting to use it. I know that's partly on me, but I wish the app was more proactive about reminding me. Like, if I haven't logged anything by noon, maybe send me a gentle reminder. Or if I'm close to my step goal, encourage me to go for a walk.

Also, the meditation logging is still confusing. I wish it was more obvious how to start and stop a session. Right now I have to dig through menus to find it."

Day 4 - March 4, 2024
Prompt: "How is the app fitting into your daily routine?"

"It's not really fitting in yet. I'm still trying to remember to use it. I think part of the problem is that it doesn't feel essential - like, I can go for a walk without the app, so why do I need it? I need to see more value.

I did check my stats today and saw that I've walked more this week than usual, which is cool. But I'm not sure if that's because of the app or just coincidence. Maybe I need to use it longer to see real benefits."

Day 5 - March 5, 2024
Prompt: "What would make you want to use this app more?"

"I think I need more motivation. Like, maybe challenges or streaks or something. Right now it's just tracking, which is fine but not exciting. I want to feel like I'm achieving something, not just logging data.

Also, I wish it connected to other apps I use. Like, if it could pull in data from my fitness tracker or my calendar, that would be helpful. Right now I'm manually entering everything, which is tedious."

Day 6 - March 6, 2024
Prompt: "How did you use the app today? Any new discoveries?"

"I discovered the social features today - you can connect with friends and see their activity. That's actually motivating. I saw that my friend Sarah walked 15,000 steps yesterday, and I was like 'I can do that too!' So I went for an extra walk in the evening.

But the social features are also a bit overwhelming. I got a bunch of friend requests from people I don't know, which feels weird. I wish I could control who can see my activity or send me requests."

Day 7 - March 7, 2024
Prompt: "One week in - how are you feeling about the app?"

"Mixed feelings. On one hand, I'm walking more and I'm more aware of my activity. On the other hand, I'm still not using all the features, and I'm not sure if the app is actually helping me or if I'm just more conscious of my habits.

I think I need to give it more time. A week isn't enough to form a habit or see real results. But I'm also starting to wonder if this is the right app for me. Maybe I need something simpler, or maybe I need something with more features. I'm not sure."

Day 8 - March 8, 2024
Prompt: "What's working well? What's not?"

"What's working: The step tracking is accurate and automatic. The notifications are helpful (when I remember to enable them). The social features are motivating.

What's not working: I still can't figure out the meditation logging. The interface is cluttered - too many options, not enough guidance. I feel like I'm using maybe 20% of what the app can do, but I don't know what the other 80% is or if I need it."

Day 9 - March 9, 2024
Prompt: "Did anything surprise you about using the app?"

"I was surprised by how much I'm walking now. I've been hitting my step goal almost every day, which I didn't expect. I think the app is making me more conscious of my activity, which is good.

But I'm also surprised by how much I'm NOT using it. Like, I forget to log meals, I forget to log sleep, I forget to meditate. The app has all these features, but I'm only using the step tracking. Maybe that's okay? Maybe I don't need all the features?"

Day 10 - March 10, 2024
Prompt: "How has your relationship with the app changed over time?"

"At first, I was excited and wanted to use all the features. Now I'm more realistic - I'm using it for what I need (step tracking) and ignoring the rest. That's probably fine, but I feel like I'm not getting the full value.

I'm also less motivated than I was at the beginning. The novelty has worn off. I need something to keep me engaged - maybe challenges, or progress toward bigger goals, or something that makes it feel like a game."

Day 11 - March 11, 2024
Prompt: "What would you tell a friend about this app?"

"I'd tell them it's good for tracking steps, but the rest is confusing. If you just want to count steps, it works fine. But if you want to use all the wellness features, be prepared for a learning curve.

I'd also say it's motivating to see your progress and connect with friends, but the social features can be overwhelming. Overall, it's okay but not amazing. I'm not sure if I'll keep using it long-term."

Day 12 - March 12, 2024
Prompt: "What's preventing you from using the app more effectively?"

"Time and mental energy. I'm busy, and remembering to log things takes effort. I wish the app could do more automatically - like, if it could detect when I'm walking or sleeping without me having to tell it, that would be better.

Also, I'm not sure what I'm supposed to be doing. Like, what's the goal? Just track things? Improve my health? The app doesn't give me clear guidance on what I should focus on or how to use it effectively."

Day 13 - March 13, 2024
Prompt: "How is the app impacting your wellness goals?"

"I'm walking more, which is good. But I'm not sure if that's because of the app or just because I'm more aware. I haven't really changed my other habits - I'm still not meditating regularly, I'm still not eating better, I'm still not sleeping more.

Maybe the app is helping with awareness, but it's not helping me actually change my behavior. I think I need more than just tracking - I need guidance, motivation, accountability. The app provides some of that, but not enough."

Day 14 - March 14, 2024
Prompt: "Final thoughts - would you continue using this app?"

"Probably, but only for step tracking. The other features aren't worth the effort for me. I'll keep using it as long as it's motivating me to walk more, but if that stops, I'll probably delete it.

I think the app has potential, but it needs to be simpler or provide more value. Right now it's trying to do too much, and as a result, it's not doing anything really well. I'd rather have a simple app that does one thing perfectly than a complex app that does many things poorly."

PARTICIPANT 5002 - Age: 35, Role: Software Engineer
================================================================================

Day 1 - March 1, 2024
Prompt: "How did you use the wellness app today? What worked well? What didn't?"

"I'm a data person, so I was excited to try this app. I set it up and connected it to my Apple Watch, which was easy. I like that it automatically tracks my activity without me having to do anything.

The interface is clean, which I appreciate. But I'm already noticing some issues - the data visualization is basic. I want to see trends over time, correlations between different metrics, that kind of thing. Right now it's just showing me daily numbers, which isn't that useful."

Day 2 - March 2, 2024
Prompt: "Did you remember to use the app? What motivated or prevented you?"

"I didn't have to remember - it's connected to my watch, so it's automatic. That's the best part. I don't want to manually log things, I want the app to do it for me.

I checked the app today and saw my activity data. It's interesting, but I want more analysis. Like, how does my activity correlate with my sleep? Or my mood? The app tracks these things separately, but doesn't show me how they're related."

Day 3 - March 3, 2024
Prompt: "What's your biggest frustration with the app so far?"

"The lack of data insights. I'm tracking all this data, but the app isn't telling me what it means. I want AI-powered insights, recommendations, predictions. Right now it's just a data logger, not a wellness coach.

Also, the export functionality is limited. I want to be able to export my data in different formats so I can analyze it myself. But the app only lets me export as CSV, and even that's clunky."

Day 4 - March 4, 2024
Prompt: "How is the app fitting into your daily routine?"

"It fits in seamlessly because it's automatic. I don't have to think about it, which is perfect. But I'm also not really engaging with it - I check it once a day, see my numbers, and move on. It's not becoming a habit or a tool I actively use."

Day 5 - March 5, 2024
Prompt: "What would make you want to use this app more?"

"Better insights and recommendations. I want the app to tell me things like 'You sleep better on days when you exercise in the morning' or 'Your step count is 20% lower on Mondays - try taking a walk during lunch.' Right now it's just showing me data without interpretation."

Day 6 - March 6, 2024
Prompt: "How did you use the app today? Any new discoveries?"

"I discovered the challenges feature today. I joined a 7-day step challenge, which is motivating. But the challenges are pretty basic - just 'walk X steps' or 'exercise Y minutes.' I want more creative challenges, or challenges based on my personal goals.

Also, I noticed the app has a premium version with more features. I'm not sure if it's worth paying for - I'd need to see what those features are and if they're actually useful."

Day 7 - March 7, 2024
Prompt: "One week in - how are you feeling about the app?"

"I'm underwhelmed. The app works, but it's not exciting or particularly useful. It's tracking my data, which is fine, but it's not helping me improve my wellness. I want more than just tracking - I want actionable insights and personalized recommendations.

I'll probably keep using it for a while to see if it gets better, but I'm not optimistic. It feels like a basic fitness tracker app, not a comprehensive wellness solution."

Day 8 - March 8, 2024
Prompt: "What's working well? What's not?"

"What's working: Automatic tracking, clean interface, easy setup.

What's not: Lack of insights, basic data visualization, no personalized recommendations, limited export options, challenges are too simple."

Day 9 - March 9, 2024
Prompt: "Did anything surprise you about using the app?"

"I was surprised by how much data I'm generating. I'm tracking steps, sleep, heart rate, workouts, meals, mood - that's a lot of data. But the app isn't doing anything interesting with it. It's just storing it. I feel like there's so much potential here that's not being realized."

Day 10 - March 10, 2024
Prompt: "How has your relationship with the app changed over time?"

"At first I was excited about the possibilities. Now I'm disappointed that it's not living up to its potential. I'm still using it, but more out of habit than enthusiasm. I check it less frequently, and I'm not really engaging with the features."

Day 11 - March 11, 2024
Prompt: "What would you tell a friend about this app?"

"I'd tell them it's fine for basic tracking, but don't expect much more. If you want detailed insights or personalized recommendations, look elsewhere. It's a decent fitness tracker, but it's not a wellness coach."

Day 12 - March 12, 2024
Prompt: "What's preventing you from using the app more effectively?"

"Nothing is preventing me - the app is easy to use. But nothing is motivating me either. I want the app to be more proactive, to give me reasons to check it, to make me want to improve. Right now it's passive, and I'm losing interest."

Day 13 - March 13, 2024
Prompt: "How is the app impacting your wellness goals?"

"Minimally. I'm aware of my activity levels, which is good, but I'm not really changing my behavior. The app isn't giving me enough motivation or guidance to actually improve. It's just showing me where I am, not helping me get where I want to be."

Day 14 - March 14, 2024
Prompt: "Final thoughts - would you continue using this app?"

"Probably not. It's not providing enough value to justify keeping it. I'll stick with my Apple Watch's built-in fitness tracking, which is simpler and does what I need. This app promised more but delivered less. I'm disappointed, but not surprised - most wellness apps are like this."
