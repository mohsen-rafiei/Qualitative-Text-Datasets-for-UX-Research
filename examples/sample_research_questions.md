# Sample Research Questions for Each Dataset

This document provides example research questions that can be answered using each dataset type. Use these as inspiration for your own research questions.

## User Interviews

### Dataset: SaaS Onboarding Interviews

**Research Questions:**

1. **Onboarding Experience:**
   - What are the main pain points users experience during onboarding?
   - How do users navigate the onboarding process?
   - What information do users need but aren't getting?
   - What causes users to feel overwhelmed during onboarding?

2. **Feature Discovery:**
   - How do users discover new features?
   - What features are users unaware of?
   - How does poor feature discovery affect user satisfaction?
   - What would help users discover features more effectively?

3. **Learning and Adaptation:**
   - How do users learn to use the product?
   - What support do users need during the learning process?
   - How long does it take users to feel comfortable?
   - What factors influence successful adaptation?

4. **Expectations vs. Reality:**
   - What do users expect from the onboarding experience?
   - How do expectations match reality?
   - What gaps exist between expectations and experience?
   - How do unmet expectations affect user satisfaction?

## General Research Question Frameworks

**For Understanding Users:**
- Who are our users?
- What are their goals and motivations?
- What problems are they trying to solve?
- How do they currently solve these problems?

**For Understanding Experiences:**
- What is the user experience like?
- What works well?
- What doesn't work well?
- How can the experience be improved?

**For Understanding Behavior:**
- How do users behave?
- What influences their behavior?
- What patterns exist?
- How can behavior be changed?

**For Understanding Needs:**
- What do users need?
- What are unmet needs?
- How do needs vary by user segment?
- How can needs be addressed?

## Tips for Writing Research Questions

1. **Be specific:** Vague questions lead to vague answers
2. **Be actionable:** Questions should lead to actionable insights
3. **Consider context:** Questions should fit the dataset and method
4. **Think about outcomes:** What decisions will these questions inform?
5. **Start broad, then narrow:** Begin with big questions, then get specific
6. **Use multiple questions:** One question rarely captures everything
7. **Consider user segments:** How might answers vary?
8. **Think about timing:** When is this question most relevant?
